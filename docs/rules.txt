21 CFR Part 11 Compliance Auditing Checklist
General Provisions
Rule 1: Applicability of Electronic Records and Signatures
Description: Electronic records and electronic signatures are considered legally equivalent to paper records and
handwritten signatures when all requirements of 21 CFR Part 11 are met.
Rationale: Ensures that electronic documentation is recognized as valid and enforceable under FDA regulations.
Measurable Criteria: System must demonstrate full compliance with all applicable sections of 21 CFR Part 11.
Evidence Requirements: Certification statements, system documentation, FDA acceptance letters.

Rule 2: Scope of Application
Description: Applies to electronic records created, modified, maintained, archived, retrieved, or transmitted under FDA
regulations.
Rationale: Clarifies the types of records and systems subject to Part 11.
Measurable Criteria: All electronic records under FDA jurisdiction must be identified and evaluated for compliance.
Evidence Requirements: Regulatory mapping, record classification documents.

Electronic Records
Rule 3: System Validation
Description: Systems managing electronic records must be validated to ensure accuracy, reliability, and consistent
performance.
Rationale: Validation ensures the integrity and reliability of electronic records.
Measurable Criteria: Documented validation protocols and test results must be available.
Evidence Requirements: Validation reports, SOPs, test logs.

Rule 4: Audit Trails
Description: Systems must generate secure, time-stamped audit trails that record all actions related to electronic
records.
Rationale: Audit trails provide accountability and traceability.
Measurable Criteria: Audit trails must be tamper-proof, retained for the record's lifecycle, and available for FDA review.
Evidence Requirements: Audit trail samples, system configuration screenshots.

Rule 5: Record Retention and Retrieval
Description: Electronic records must be protected and retrievable throughout their retention period.
Rationale: Ensures records are accessible for audits and inspections.
Measurable Criteria: Secure storage and retrieval mechanisms must be in place.
Evidence Requirements: Archive access logs, retrieval test reports.

Electronic Signatures
Rule 6: Uniqueness of Electronic Signatures
Description: Each electronic signature must be unique to one individual and not reused or reassigned.
Rationale: Prevents impersonation and ensures accountability.
Measurable Criteria: Maintain a registry of electronic signatures and enforce uniqueness.
Evidence Requirements: Signature assignment records, user ID logs.

Rule 7: Signature Manifestations
Description: Signed electronic records must include the signer's name, date/time of signing, and meaning of the
signature.
Rationale: Provides context and traceability for each signature.
Measurable Criteria: All signed records must display required metadata in human-readable form.
Evidence Requirements: Signed record samples, metadata logs.

Rule 8: Signature/Record Linking
Description: Electronic signatures must be linked to their respective records to prevent removal or falsification.
Rationale: Maintains the integrity of signed records.
Measurable Criteria: Technical controls must bind signatures to records securely.
Evidence Requirements: System design documentation, linkage test results.

Rule 9: Access Control
Description: System access must be limited to authorized individuals using secure authentication.
Rationale: Prevents unauthorized access and protects data integrity.
Measurable Criteria: Use role-based access control and maintain access logs.
Evidence Requirements: Access control policies, user access logs.

Rule 10: Identification Code and Password Security
Description: Identification codes and passwords must be unique, periodically reviewed, and protected against
unauthorized use.
Rationale: Ensures secure authentication and prevents misuse.
Measurable Criteria: Implement password aging, loss management, and monitoring for unauthorized access.
Evidence Requirements: Security policies, password audit reports.

Rule 11: Training and Accountability
Description: Personnel must be trained and held accountable for actions taken under their electronic signatures.
Rationale: Ensures proper system use and deters misuse or falsification.
Measurable Criteria: Maintain training records and enforce written accountability policies.
Evidence Requirements: Training logs, signed accountability agreements.
